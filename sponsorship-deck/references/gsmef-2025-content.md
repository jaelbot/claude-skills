## Theme

**Theme**: Building Resilience and Creating Opportunities for Growth

> "MSMEs are faced with numerous challenges and are most exposed to external shocks (notably recent ones like COVID, trade tariffs, and global supply chain disruptions) and macroeconomic shifts. Especially, in today's volatile global landscape, the scale and frequency of these external shocks have increased. These disruptions have widened the MSME finance gap from $4.4 trillion to $5.7 trillion, inhibiting the growth of MSMEs and their ability to sustain and create jobs.
>
> Research shows that every additional $1 million in SME financing in developing countries creates an average of 16 new jobs over two years. With limited access to funding, resource constraints and skill gaps, SMEs need tailored, innovative and shock-proof financial tools and strategic solutions that make them resilient."

## Topics

### Pillar 1: Improving Market Access
- Leveraging technology with appropriate risk management frameworks, emphasising importance of financial health and impact pathways
- Optimising value chain integration and building cross-border digital infrastructure to facilitate seamless participation in global markets
- Enabling new corridors for financial flows

### Pillar 2: Developing Entrepreneurial Skills
- Building resiliency by fostering digital literacy, data-driven decision-making, and entrepreneurial mindsets
- Adopting digital solutions to drive efficiency, agility, and competitiveness
- Scaling successful approaches from leading entrepreneurs and building public-private partnerships

### Pillar 3: Access to Funding and New Financial Instruments
- Harnessing fintech innovations and non-bank lending solutions to close credit gaps and reach underserved SME segments, and role of development actors and sovereign wealth funds
- Developing robust digital public infrastructure to lower barriers and costs for SME financing
- Enabling regulatory frameworks that support new financial instruments and local currency solutions
